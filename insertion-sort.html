Insertion Sort

Description

Insertion Sort is a simple yet effective sorting algorithm that is widely used in computer science education to teach sorting fundamentals. It builds the final sorted array one item at a time, making it efficient for small datasets and partially sorted arrays.

  • How It Works: The algorithm iteratively takes one element from the unsorted portion and inserts it into its correct position in the sorted portion.
  • Time Complexity: Best Case - O(n), Average and Worst Case - O(nÂ²). The efficiency is highest when the data is nearly sorted or the dataset is small.
  • Usage Scenarios: Ideal for situations where simplicity is key or when dealing with small to moderately sized datasets. It's also efficient for nearly sorted data.
  • Advantages and Disadvantages: Advantages include ease of implementation and efficiency on small or nearly sorted datasets. However, it becomes inefficient as the size of the dataset grows.
